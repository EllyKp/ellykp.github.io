From barn records to breakthrough insights

Research Overview

I turn the messy, longitudinal data of commercial livestock production into analytics that matter — to the producer making culling decisions at 5 a.m., to the company modeling profitability across 10,000 sows, and to the scientist building the next generation of precision animal health tools. My work lives at the intersection of epidemiology, applied machine learning, and production economics, with a singular focus: translate numbers into decisions.

Research Vision

Commercial swine production generates extraordinary amounts of data — every breeding event, every litter born, every sow removed records a signal. Yet most of that signal stays trapped in software dashboards, never translated into predictive intelligence. My research challenges that gap.

I ask: What does the data already know that the producer doesn't?

Working with large-scale, longitudinal production datasets, I build models that identify which sows are at risk before they fail, which gilts will stay productive through multiple parities, and which management decisions yield the greatest return under real-world economic constraints. The analytics I develop are not academic exercises — they are designed to be used, understood, and acted upon at the barn level.

🔬 Research Pillars
📈

Longitudinal Performance Modeling

Modeling sow- and parity-specific trajectories over time to predict reproductive outcomes, longevity, and lifetime productivity — treating production records as the time-series data they truly are.

🤖

Explainable Machine Learning

Developing transparent, interpretable ML models that translate complex multivariate patterns into producer-ready decision rules — prediction with purpose, not just prediction with accuracy.

🌽

Production Economics & Decision Support

Partial budgeting, scenario simulation, and economic modeling of sow removal, replacement, and retention decisions — quantifying the dollar value of better data-driven choices.

🛡️

Epidemiology & Sentinel Surveillance

Designing anomaly detection pipelines using reproductive and performance indicators to flag herd deviations early — surveillance that protects both animal health and economic outcomes.

🧬

One Health & Antimicrobial Stewardship

Bridging animal production, food safety, and public health through resistance profiling, milk quality analytics, and evidence-based frameworks for antimicrobial use on-farm.

⚙️ Current & Recent Projects
PhD Dissertation · Swine Efficiency

Applied Data Analytics for Breeding Herd Efficiency in Commercial Swine Production

A longitudinal, multi-parity analysis of sow performance trajectories to identify biological and management drivers of farrowing success, retention, and lifetime economic return. Uses mixed models, survival analysis, and ML classifiers on commercial herd data.

● Active — Exp. 2027
Funded · Pig Livability Grant

Predicting Farrowing Success to Inform Breeding Decisions

Building ML models that classify risk of farrowing failure at the individual sow level prior to breeding — enabling producers to make proactive, data-backed decisions rather than reactive post-failure ones. Presented at AI in Veterinary Medicine Symposium (2026).

● Funded · $30,000
Funded · Pig Livability Grant

Early Identification of High-Risk Piglets in Commercial Swine Systems

Developing algorithms to flag at-risk piglets in the first days of life using routinely collected production indicators. Targets mortality reduction by shifting from reactive intervention to early, targeted management.

● Funded · $30,000
Funded · ISU VPR

Precision Sow Management System (PSMS)

A platform integrating real-time production data with predictive modeling to support breeding, nutrition, and culling decisions at scale. Connects multiple analytical modules into a unified decision-support framework for commercial operations.

● Funded · $50,000
Under Review · USDA-CARE

Unravelling Predictors of Sow Retention, Productivity & Profitability

A comprehensive study linking parity-level performance metrics, management variables, and economic indicators to identify what actually drives long-term sow value — and what separates a sustainable producer from a struggling one.

● Under Review · $300,000
Real-Time Surveillance · Pig Livability

Mortality Surveillance System for Wean-to-Finish Lots

Building real-time anomaly detection pipelines that use mortality patterns and health indicators to flag emerging disease events — transforming reactive loss reporting into proactive herd health intelligence.
